Your AGI will be found on line 11 of your 2024 Form 1040, 1040-SR, and 1040-NR. If you plan to e-file your tax return, you may need to first find the amount of AGI from last year's return in order for the IRS to verify your identity.You can find your AGI on the form you used to file your last year's return.

    • The employer identification number is a unique nine-digit number assigned by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to business entities operating in the United States for the purpose of identification.

    In the context of the United States, "SSN" stands for Social Security Number — a unique nine-digit number assigned to U.S. citizens and residents for tracking income and determining benefits.

    A Zipcode is a group of five or nine numbers that are added to a postal address to assist the sorting of mail.

    "State wages, tips" is the amount of money you earned in your state (like from your job), including: Your regular pay (wages), tips you reported, any other taxable income from your employer. This is the money that your state uses to calculate state income tax.

    Nonqualified plans are retirement or deferred compensation plans that don’t meet IRS requirements for favorable tax treatment. Employers report distributions from these plans in Box 11.

    • Statutory employee: Your earnings are subject to Social Security and Medicare but not federal income tax withholding.
    • Retirement plan: You participated in your employer’s retirement plan.
    • Third-party sick pay: You received payments for sick leave through an insurance provider or third party.

    Box 14 is used for miscellaneous information not reported elsewhere on the W-2, such as union dues, health insurance premiums, or vehicle allowances. The exact meaning depends on what your employer puts there!

    Box 12 contains letter-coded information for specific types of compensation or deductions, such as:
    • Code D: Contributions to a 401(k) plan
    • Code DD: Cost of employer-sponsored health coverage
    • ...and many more. Check the IRS instructions or your tax preparer for details.
