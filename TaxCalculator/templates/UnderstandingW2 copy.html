Understanding Your W-2

A guide to what your W-2 means and how to use it.

A W-2 is a tax form that employers send to employees, summarizing annual earnings and tax withholdings.

  • Wages & Salaries: Your total earnings before taxes.
  • Federal Income Tax Withheld: Taxes deducted by your employer.
  • Social Security & Medicare: Contributions deducted from your wages.
  • Employer & Employee Info: Identifying details like your SSN and your employerâ€™s EIN.

Employers must send W-2 forms by January 31 each year for the previous tax year.

You use your W-2 to file your tax return (Form 1040). It helps determine if you owe taxes or get a refund.
