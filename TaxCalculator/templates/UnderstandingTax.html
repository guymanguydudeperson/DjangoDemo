Understanding Taxes

A brief guide on how to file your taxes and more.

  • Gather your documents - You'll need your W-2 (or 1099 if you're a contractor), last year's tax return, and any deduction-related paperwork.
  • Choose how to file - You can use online tax software, hire a professional, or file manually using IRS forms.
  • Complete your tax return - Enter your income, deductions, and credits to determine your tax refund or what you owe.
  • Submit before the deadline - The tax filing deadline is usually April 15. File electronically for faster processing.

    A refund is money the IRS returns if you paid more in taxes than you owed. You can get it faster by filing electronically and choosing direct deposit.

If you miss the April 15 deadline, you may face penalties and interest on unpaid taxes. File an extension if needed, but pay what you owe to avoid extra fees.

Your employer withholds taxes from your paycheck based on your W-4 form. If too much is withheld, you get a refund. If too little, you may owe taxes.
